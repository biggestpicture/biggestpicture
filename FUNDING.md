# Funding policy

This project scores state control and surveillance. Its own funding sources
are therefore held to a stricter standard than a typical open source
project — the goal is to never end up in the position we flagged as a
caveat against one of our own source datasets (Carnegie Endowment's
disclosed funders include cloud/AI-adjacent firms).

## Rules

1. **Public ledger.** Every funder contributing more than $500 (cumulative,
   trailing 12 months) is listed below with amount, date, and stated
   purpose. Updated on receipt, not retroactively.
2. **Hard cap.** No single funder — corporate, foundation, or government —
   may exceed **20% of trailing-12-month funding**. If a donation would
   cross that line, the excess is declined or redirected.
3. **Excluded funders, no exceptions:**
   - Surveillance-technology vendors (facial recognition, spyware,
     government surveillance contractors, data-broker firms selling to
     state actors).
   - Intelligence agencies or their contractors.
   - State-run media or propaganda bodies.
   - Political parties, campaigns, or PACs.
4. **Funding review is public before acceptance.** Any offer above the
   disclosure threshold is posted as an issue for open comment before
   being accepted, not accepted quietly and disclosed later.
5. **No paid editorial control.** Funding pays for hosting, IPFS pinning,
   and infrastructure only. No funder gets a say in scoring methodology,
   axis weighting, or which countries are included — that's governed
   entirely by `GOVERNANCE.md`.

## Preferred funding, in order

1. Individual small donors (GitHub Sponsors, Open Collective) — structurally
   hardest to capture.
2. Academic or research grants with no strings attached.
3. Civil-liberties-focused foundations — subject to the same disclosure and
   the same 20% cap as everyone else.
