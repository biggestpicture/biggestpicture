Privacy & VPNs

VPNs actually built for privacy — not just marketed that way

Most "private" VPNs are mainstream ad-driven businesses with a privacy label. A few are actually built for it.

We researched ownership structure, independent audits, and real controversies for five well-known providers, plus two genuinely privacy-first alternatives. The split doesn't track affiliate payout — the providers most worth recommending on principle pay us the least, or nothing at all, and we think that's worth telling you rather than hiding.

If surveillance-freedom is your actual concern

No email required to sign up, cash/Monero accepted as first-class payment, and — tellingly — both refuse affiliate marketing on principle.

Mullvad Top pick

Sweden · €5/month flat, no tiers, no renewal price hikes

No email or identity required — you get a 16-digit account number instead. Accepts cash by mail, Bitcoin, Bitcoin Cash, and Monero. Repeatedly audited by Cure53 with no critical findings. Widely regarded as the industry's privacy benchmark.

Worth knowing: Sweden is a member of the "14 Eyes" surveillance-sharing alliance — the one real jurisdictional caveat here — though Mullvad's no-identity account system means there's structurally very little for authorities to compel disclosure of, even under legal order.

IVPN Top pick

Gibraltar · from $6/month

Same no-email, account-number signup model as Mullvad. Cure53-audited annually since 2019, fully open-source clients. Accepts cash by mail, Bitcoin, and Monero.

Worth knowing: IVPN used to run an affiliate program and shut it down entirely in 2024 after finding under 5% of affiliates were complying with disclosure requirements — they'd rather have no affiliate revenue than tolerate undisclosed paid promotion. That's the kind of decision we think is worth rewarding with a recommendation, not penalizing for the lack of a commission.

Solid fundamentals, mainstream business model

Proton VPN

Switzerland · from $2.99/month (2-year plan) · genuine free tier available

Independently audited by Securitum four years running (2022–2025). Swiss jurisdiction, outside the major surveillance-sharing alliances. Accepts cash by mail alongside cards and Bitcoin — rare among mainstream providers.

Worth knowing: Proton Mail (a related product, not the VPN itself) has twice disclosed user-identifying metadata under legal compulsion — once to Swiss authorities in a 2021 criminal case, once assisting Spanish police in 2024. Neither incident involved the VPN's core connection logs, and Proton VPN's own no-logs claims remain independently audited and unchallenged — but it's relevant context about the parent organization.

The ExpressVPN note, in full

ExpressVPN's CIO, Daniel Gericke, previously worked on the UAE's "Project Raven" — a 2016–2019 state hacking program that built zero-click phone exploits used against journalists, activists, and dissidents. Gericke and two co-defendants reached a Justice Department deferred prosecution agreement and paid a combined $1.6 million fine. ExpressVPN has said it hired him knowing this history, which drew public criticism, including from Edward Snowden. This is a direct thematic conflict for a site built around freedom from surveillance to gloss over — ExpressVPN's technical audits are genuine, but that's a separate question from who's running the company.

Why "audited" alone isn't the full picture

  • All of NordVPN, Surfshark, and ExpressVPN are competently audited and technically sound VPNs. Audits verify no-logs architecture — they don't tell you who owns the company or what else that owner does.
  • None of the three mainstream options accept cash. All three have the highest affiliate payouts of any provider researched — worth factoring in when a "top 5 VPN" list conveniently only features high-commission providers.
  • If privacy from state/institutional surveillance specifically (not just Netflix region-unlocking) is your actual goal, Mullvad or IVPN's no-identity account model is a structurally stronger guarantee than any audit of a company that still knows your email and payment history.
