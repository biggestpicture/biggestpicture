The Biggest Picture

Global State Control & Surveillance Heat Map

Two independent datasets: a composite heat map blending three published indices into one directional score per country, and Primary Questions — 9 concrete, plain-language questions per country, each answer cited to a source you can check yourself, built entirely through open contribution. Named for the fable of the frog in slowly heating water — each individual measure looks survivable up close; the point of this project is to step back far enough to see the whole pot. Not a published index — read the methodology before citing either dataset.

Methodology, sources & limitations (short version)

Sources

  • AI Surveillance — Carnegie Endowment, AI Global Surveillance (AIGS) Index, 2022 update (Feldstein). Score = share of four tracked surveillance categories documented as deployed, scaled 0–100. Only countries with ≥1 documented category are scored; the rest show "no data," not zero.
  • Movement & Regulatory Restriction — Human Freedom Index 2023 edition (Cato/Fraser), 2021 data. Average of pf_movement and ef_regulation sub-indices, inverted so higher = more restricted.
  • Digital Surveillance & Censorship — Feldstein/Carnegie Digital Repression Index 2021 (V-Dem-derived), min-max normalized 0–100.

Primary Questions (second dataset)

9 factual questions per country, community-sourced rather than borrowed from an existing index — each answer graded yes/partial/no/unclear with a mandatory note and citation, validated in CI. Deliberately not blended into a score. See data/questions/questions.json for the full grading rubric and CONTRIBUTING.md to add a country.

Full methodology, including composite calculation, known limitations, funding-conflict disclosure for source data, and the data-corruption fix applied during ingestion, lives in docs/methodology.md — linked below.
