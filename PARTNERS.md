# Partner & Affiliate Policy

This governs everything published on [SolutionWatch](site/solutionwatch/) —
the vetting standard, the commercial terms, and the review process. It
exists because a page that recommends real services people pay real money
for needs a public standard *before* the first listing goes live, not a
promise added after the fact. See `site/solutionwatch/index.html` for how
this page is kept structurally separate from the Composite Index and
Primary Questions data — that separation is the precondition for this
policy meaning anything.

## What qualifies for a listing

All of the following, not some of them:

1. **Directly relevant** — the service has to actually help someone reduce
   their exposure to state surveillance or control: privacy tooling,
   encrypted communications, alternative banking, residency/mobility
   options, asset diversification, and similar. "Good company, unrelated
   product" doesn't qualify.
2. **Independently investigated, not just self-submitted** — a listing
   requires someone on this project to have actually used the service or
   done real due diligence (searched for complaints, checked regulatory
   status where one exists, verified the business is real and operating) —
   not a summary of the partner's own marketing copy.
3. **Transparent pricing** — publicly stated pricing or a clear, public
   price range. No listings for services that only reveal cost after a
   sales call.
4. **No unverifiable or illegal guarantees** — a service promising a
   "guaranteed" citizenship outcome, guaranteed tax elimination, or
   guaranteed anonymity where the underlying legal or technical reality is
   actually uncertain does not qualify, regardless of commission offered.
   Higher-stakes categories (offshore banking, citizenship/residency,
   legal restructuring) get proportionally more scrutiny than a privacy
   browser extension.
5. **Legally operating** in the jurisdictions it serves.

## Automatically excluded

No exceptions, regardless of commission offered:

- Services that themselves enable surveillance, tracking, or reporting on
  individuals — the obvious conflict with this project's actual purpose.
- MLM or pyramid-structured offerings.
- Predatory lending, debt, or "guaranteed return" investment products.
- Anything making a legal/outcome guarantee that isn't actually
  guaranteed (see above).
- Anything the vetting reviewer can't verify is a real, operating
  business.

## Commercial terms

- **Every listing is labeled** — "Affiliate partner" or "No commercial
  relationship," visibly, on the listing itself. Non-affiliate listings
  (a genuinely good free/independent tool we just think belongs here) are
  explicitly allowed and encouraged — the page isn't required to be 100%
  monetized, and a page that's only ever paid listings is a warning sign
  about itself.
- **No partner buys placement, ranking, or editorial framing.** Commission
  rate has no bearing on how prominently something is listed or how it's
  described.
- **Revenue concentration cap** — no single partner may account for more
  than **30% of trailing-12-month SolutionWatch affiliate revenue.** If
  one partner's revenue share would cross that line, additional referral
  traffic to them is capped, not the listing pulled — the point is
  avoiding a single partner's revenue becoming leverage over whether they
  stay listed despite quality problems.
- **Aggregate revenue disclosed periodically** — see the ledger at the
  bottom of this document, same spirit as `FUNDING.md`'s public funder
  ledger. Per-partner exact commission figures aren't published (that's
  between us and the partner), but aggregate SolutionWatch income is.

## Review process before a listing goes live

1. Whoever is proposing a partner opens a PR adding the listing (structure
   TBD — will follow the same one-file-per-entry pattern as
   `data/questions/countries/` once the first real candidate exists),
   including what was checked under "independently investigated" above.
2. **14-day public comment period** — same window used for composite-axis
   RFCs in `GOVERNANCE.md`. This is where outside readers who've had a bad
   experience with a proposed partner get a chance to say so before it's
   live, not after.
3. **Sign-off from two maintainers from different countries or
   institutions** — same anti-capture rule that governs composite-index
   changes in `GOVERNANCE.md`, applied here for the same reason: one
   person shouldn't be able to unilaterally decide what gets recommended
   to readers.
4. Merged listings are logged in the changelog below with the date and
   who reviewed it.

## Ongoing review and removal

- Every active listing is re-reviewed **at least annually** — a service
  that was legitimate at listing time can change practices, get
  acquired, or accumulate complaints.
- **Any maintainer can propose removal at any time** if evidence of a
  problem surfaces; removal doesn't wait for the annual cycle.
- Removals are logged publicly with a brief, honest reason — quietly
  dropping a partner without explanation is exactly the kind of opacity
  this whole policy exists to avoid.
- No partner has a contractual right to indefinite listing. The
  affiliate agreement is revenue-sharing on referrals; it is not
  editorial control, and it doesn't purchase permanence.

## Editorial independence

Partners — current or prospective — get no access to, preview of, or
influence over the Composite Index or Primary Questions datasets, and no
input into scoring methodology. This is the same firewall `FUNDING.md`
maintains for the project's own funders, applied to commercial partners.
